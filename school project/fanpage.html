Tribute to Frank A. Sinatra

Biography:

Francis Albert Sinatra (/sɪˈnɑːtrə/; December 12, 1915 – May 14, 1998) was an American singer and actor who is generally perceived
as one of the greatest musical artists of the 20th century.
He is one of the best-selling music artists of all time, having sold an estimated 150 million records worldwide.
Born to Italian immigrants in Hoboken, New Jersey, Sinatra was greatly influenced by the intimate,
easy-listening vocal style of Bing Crosby
and began his musical career in the swing era with bandleaders Harry James and Tommy Dorsey.
